Instructor's checklist to launch a new EI cohort

Day 1 morning:

In addition to presenting slides, in morning workshop: 

  • Check that students can create tickets by typing the /create-ticket command in the support-ei-cohortXX channel
  • Ask students to let you know if they receive an error message, and what that error message is.
  • If any students cannot create tickets, let Emma Holland know and she will fix the issue within the first day.

Be prepared to demo:

  • mkdir
  • cd
  • pwd
  • touch index.html
  • open VSCode (code in terminal)
  • Update slide 8 of first day course structure slides with new Github Org name (ex: quail): https://github.com/thinkful-ei-quail
  • Create Github Org thinkful-ei-[animal-codename]  (each cohort gets a new Github org - here is the former cohort's org: https://github.com/thinkful-ei-panda).  The codename is an animal of your choice, alphabetically in sequence from last, e.g. thinkful-ei-cheetah, thinkful-ei-dragonfly)
  • Add EI instructors and TAs as owners to Github org (TA's: ESM Synchronous Operations: All Course Cohort Starts & Staffing)
  • Add all students (see list here: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1SLQIdtcTj6qNUxcZeHdfMe4C7yHqGglxyIxnJ8HjtCU/)
  • Pin starter repos to Github Org (see https://github.com/thinkful-ei-panda):
    • jQuery-playground
    • quiz-app-boilerplate (this is the starter code for the Quiz App Assignments and it's files contain the precise instructions for how they should build their Quiz Apps using state-based architecture.
    • github-fetch-demo
    • github-pages-and-formData
    • shopping-list-starter (master & with-store branches) - This is created from the repo here that contains solutions for each shopping list assignment.

    Here are the days that each additional solution branch should be posted to the Github Org:

    • Week 2, Thurs @ 12:45pm ET: complex-ui-store-solution
    • Week 2, Friday @ 12:45pm ET: with-modules
    • Week 3, Mon am (before class): with-webpack
    • Week 3, Wed am: with-api-webpack
    • Week 3, Wed am: with-api-webpack
    • Review curriculum - especially looking for any public API's used in the curriculum that may have changed since the last cohort (to ensure the curriculum instructions still work)
    • Generate Syllabus (See instructions here: https://github.com/Thinkful-Ed/ft-syllabus-generator)
    • Create Student Health Sheet (example here)
    • Link Student Health Sheet sheet to staff channel as the topic
    • Post welcome message to Slack as soon as the students have been added to the channel by Emma Holland and syllabus has been generated.

    The welcome message should include instructions to complete their prework and accept their Github Org invites.

    Here is an example:

    Hello and welcome to Engineering Immersion! I will be your instructor for the first 3 weeks of your program. The first week of your syllabus has now been posted, and you may find it by looking at your student dashboards.

    Please pay special attention to all items posted on Sunday's dashboard, including opening and reading the absence form which contains details of Thinkful's absence policy throughout your course.

    There are some required environment setup installations that must be completed prior to your first day (listed on your dashboards). Please be sure to allow yourself enough time to complete these items, and to reach out for help if you run into trouble.

    In addition to completing your environment installations,please accept your invitations to join your cohort's Github Organization at https://github.com/thinkful-ei-panda.

    If you don't see your invite, it may be in your spam folder. You can also directly accept your invitation at https://github.com/thinkful-ei-panda.

    If you cannot locate your invite or are unable to join the organization - please respond to this message by sending me your Github handle.

    I look forward to meeting you all on Monday!
